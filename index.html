About me

Dalton Mooring is a classical tenor trombonist and physics student currently attending the University of Ottawa. Expected to graduate in 2021, he is currently enrolled in a double degree program working towards a degree in trombone performance and an honors degree in physics. After graduating Dalton will look to perform with various musical ensembles and do research into topics related to quantum physics and particle physics.
When he is not practicing orchestral excerpts and pondering why quantum chromodynamics does not seemingly break charge parity symmetry principles he is looking up new vegan recipes, running or playing video games with his friends.

Performance

Dalton has been playing trombone for ten years. He has received numerous awards for solo as well as ensemble performance. Click here here to view a full music résumé. He has a background playing in many different larger ensembles including drum corps, wind ensembles, symphonic orchestras, and more. In addition he has played in smaller groups such as pit orchestras, brass quintets, and jazz combos and others.

History

Dalton has also been exposed to the another side of music, often taking the role as principle, first chair, section leader which has granted him valuable time to grow as a leader and pass his skills on to others. In addition to being a group leader Dalton enjoys composing original pieces, arranging pieces for both small and large groups and sometimes teaching and conducting these pieces for performance in concert.
Currently Dalton is a member of several performing ensembles at the University of Ottawa including the wind ensemble and orchestra. He is under the guidance of Eastman graduate and NACO bass trombone Douglas Burden.
